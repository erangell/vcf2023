Identifying modified Group 1 intervals

It is easy to identify perfect intervals. If the note that is a fifth above the bottom note is in the major scale or key signature of the bottom note, then it is perfect. But what if the fifth is not perfect?

Below is the interval from Eb to B-natural.

To identify this interval, the first step is to determine the general size by counting lines and spaces. We see that the interval is a fifth of some kind. The second step is to think about the major scale or major key signature of the bottom note of the interval, Eb in this example. The key signature of Eb major is Bb, Eb, and Ab. Therefore, we know that Bb is part of the Eb major scale and that Eb to Bb would be the perfect fifth. But in this case, the Bb is raised a half step to B natural, making the interval augmented. So Eb-B is an augmented fifth (A5).
