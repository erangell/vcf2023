TRIAD EAR-TRAINING

In this module, you will learn to distinguish between major, minor, augmented and diminished  triads by ear. The best way to accomplish this task is to listen for the quality of the third between the root and the third and then to listen for the quality between the root and the fifth. Click on the example below and listen closely to the character of the sound.

You should have heard that the interval between the root and the third (in this case C to E) was a major third. You should have heard that the interval between the root and the fifth (C to G) was a perfect fifth. As you learned in the Diatonic Triads module, a triad with a major third between the root and the third and a perfect fifth between the root and the fifth is a major triad. Play the example above again until you are satisfied that the sound of a major triad is in your tonal memory.

You should have heard that the interval between the root and the third is a major third and that the interval between the root and the fifth is an augmented fifth. The combination of these intervals forms an augmented triad. Listen again until you have memorized the sound of this triad.
