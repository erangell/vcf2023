SOUND CHECK: Macintosh Computers

To check your sound settings on a Macintosh computer, look for the "System Preferences" icon in the tray at the bottom of the screen. (You can also find it in the Applications folder on your Hard Drive.) When you open System Preferences, you will see a screen similar to the one below.



Locate the "Sound" icon and open it. (In the picture above, it is the last icon on the right of the second row.) You will then see the screen below:



At this screen, you want to make sure that the Output Volume is set to a level you can hear, and the Mute checkbox is Unchecked. You should also test your Microphone and adjust the Input Volume so that the graph shows between 50% and 75% when you sing a note very loudly. If you are in a noisy environment, check the box labelled "Use ambient noise reduction".

(Note: This course contains sight-singing exercises in which a quiet environment is necessary for the computer to properly determine the pitch you sing).

When you are done changing the Sound settings, exit System Preferences.
